# Atlassian · Sprint Retrospectives

**Source**: https://www.atlassian.com/agile/scrum/retrospectives

## Key takeaways

- Retros are a **Scrum ceremony** held after every sprint review and before sprint planning. They isolate "what happened" from "what's next."
- Recommended cadence: **once per sprint** (1–4 weeks). Quarterly retros at the team/org level catch trends single sprints miss.
- Recommended duration: **45–60 minutes** for a 2-week sprint; scale linearly.
- Three buckets that drive most teams: **What went well**, **What didn't**, **What we'll change**. Add a 4th bucket — **Action items with owners** — to move from talk to commitment.
- The facilitator's job is to make the meeting safe and bounded, not to lead opinions. Rotate facilitation to avoid bias.
- Anti-patterns Atlassian calls out: meetings without actions, blame culture, and skipping retros under deadline pressure (precisely when they're most needed).

## Why authoritative

Atlassian sells Jira/Confluence to ~80% of Fortune 500 — their retrospective guide is the de-facto reference used across the Scrum/agile industry and is regularly updated.
